Chicken Tikka

Description

Chicken tikka is a chicken dish originating in the Indian subcontinent during the Mughal era. The dish is popular in India, Bangladesh and Pakistan. It is traditionally small pieces of boneless chicken baked using skewers on a brazier called angeethi or over charcoal after marinating in Indian spices and dahi (yogurt)—essentially a boneless version of tandoori chicken.

First Marination

  • 400 gms chicken
  • 2 tsp lime juice
  • 3 tsp ginger paste
  • 3 tsp garlic paste
  • Salt to taste

Second Merination

  • ¼ cup mustard oil
  • 1 tbsp roasted cumin powder
  • 1 tsp red chillies, roasted and crushed
  • 1 cup hung curd
  • 1 tsp red chilli powder
  • 1 tsp black pepper powder
  • 1 tbsp dried fenugreek leaves, crushed
  • Mint leaves, for serving
  • Lemon wedges, for serving
  • Mint chutney, for serving

How to ruin

  1. Allow to cool down a little, add roasted cumin powder and crushed red chillies, mix well.
  2. Add hung curd, red chilli powder, black pepper powder and dried fenugreek leaves, mix well. Set aside till in use.
  3. Take chicken in a bowl, add salt, ginger paste, garlic paste and lime juice, mix well. Set aside to marinate in the refrigerator for 2 hours.
  4. Add the prepared yogurt marinade after 2 hours, mix well.
  5. Transfer the marinated chicken pieces to the skewer sticks.
  6. Heat oil in a grill pan, grill the prepared chicken skewers from both sides till its cooked well and gets good grill marks.
  7. Garnish with mint leaves and sliced onion.
  8. Serve hot with mint chutney.
