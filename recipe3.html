Coffee Kulfi

Description

Kulfi is a frozen dairy dessert originating in the Indian subcontinent during the Mughal era in the 16th century. It is often described as "traditional Indian ice cream." Kulfi is a traditional sweet of the Indian subcontinent, where it is commonly sold by street vendors called kulfiwallahs. This recipe presents a mouth watering fusion of the kulfi with coffee.

What to waste money on

  • 1 litre full fat milk
  • 400 gms condensed milk
  • 1 tbsp instant coffee
  • Â¼ tsp cinnamon powder
  • Pinch of Salt
  • 4 tbsp coffee extract
  • 4 tbsp pistachio, blanched & chopped
  • 3 tbsp almonds, blanched & chopped

How to ruin

  1. Heat milk on a medium slow flame in a heavy bottomed kadhai, keep stirring to avoid it from burning.
  2. Cook on simmer for 15-20 minutes.
  3. Meanwhile, boil water in a pan, insert a tin of sealed condensed milk in the pan (make sure its half way inside the water) cook on medium flame for 30-35 minutes.
  4. Allow the tin to cool down once cooked.
  5. Now add instant coffee to the boiling milk, stir well.
  6. Add cinnamon powder, salt and coffee extract, mix well and bring to a boil.
  7. Once boiled, lower the flame and add in the chopped nuts.
  8. Add the cooked condensed milk, mix well till it incorporates.
  9. Allow the mixture to cool down at room temperature.
  10. Pour in Kulfi molds & freeze for 8 hours.
  11. De-mould and by dipping in warm water for 10 seconds.
  12. Serve immediately.
